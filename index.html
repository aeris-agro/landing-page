01The Problem

Uganda's agricultural sector employs over 70% of the population and contributes approximately 24% of GDP — yet it is systemically underperforming. Northern Uganda's Lango sub-region, home to roughly 120,000 smallholder farming households, loses an estimated $445 million in value annually to a convergence of preventable failures: post-harvest crop losses, price opacity, input misapplication, and the near-total absence of structured market access for small producers.

"This is not a market failure. It is an intelligence failure. Farmers cannot make optimal decisions without timely, localized, trustworthy data. Without that data, every step of the agricultural cycle — from input procurement to post-harvest sale — is a guess."

The root causes are structural and mutually reinforcing. Farmers lack access to agronomic advisory tailored to their specific fields. Buyers cannot verify crop quality or provenance. Middlemen extract disproportionate margin by exploiting information asymmetry. And government agencies lack granular, real-time data to deploy support interventions effectively.

Existing solutions address these challenges in isolation — an SMS advisory service here, a mobile money wallet there — but no platform has yet closed the loop from field intelligence to market transaction in a single integrated system.

02Our Solution — The AERIS Ecosystem

AERIS Agro has designed a closed-loop agricultural intelligence platform built on three interconnected products. Together, they move data from the field to the market — and back — creating a feedback system that improves with every season.

Aeryion — Field Intelligence
A satellite and drone-assisted remote sensing platform that generates hyper-local vegetation indices, soil moisture estimates, and pest-risk alerts for individual farm plots. District agricultural officers and extension workers access a web dashboard; farmers receive push alerts via Coltiva. Powered by Google Earth Engine and a Supabase-backed geospatial data pipeline.
Coltiva — Farmer Interface
A USSD and SMS advisory layer that requires no smartphone or data connection. Farmers query Coltiva for planting schedules, input recommendations, weather outlooks, and real-time market prices. A freemium model provides basic alerts at no cost; premium advisory — including weekly agronomist-reviewed farm reports — is available for UGX 5,000/month (approx. $1.30).
LinkTrade — Market Access
A digital commodity marketplace connecting smallholder farmers directly to verified buyers — processors, exporters, NGOs, and institutional purchasers. Transactions are secured through mobile-money escrow, eliminating payment risk for both parties. Standardized grading protocols built into the platform ensure buyers get consistent quality and farmers get fair prices — with full transaction traceability.

The three platforms are designed to be mutually reinforcing. Aeryion data improves the advisory quality of Coltiva. Coltiva engagement drives LinkTrade adoption by keeping farmers active on the platform through the growing season. LinkTrade transaction data feeds back into Aeryion's demand-side forecasting, creating a self-improving intelligence cycle.

03Market Opportunity

Immediate addressable market

The Lango sub-region — comprising Lira, Alebtong, Dokolo, and surrounding districts — represents our launch geography. With approximately 120,000 smallholder farming households and an average farm size of 2–5 acres, the region produces primarily maize, cassava, sunflower, and sorghum. The combined gross value of Lango's agricultural output is estimated at over $800 million annually, of which post-harvest losses consume more than half.

Competitive landscape

Existing players in Uganda's agritech space operate in narrow verticals: ENSIBUUKO focuses on SACCO digitisation; Kyagalanyi Coffee operates single-commodity supply chains; Apollo Agriculture (Kenya) provides input financing. No competitor offers the full intelligence-to-market loop that AERIS provides, nor do any operate a USSD-first interface paired with satellite field monitoring at the farm-plot level.

Our primary moat is data network effects: every additional farmer using Coltiva enriches the Aeryion model; every additional buyer on LinkTrade improves price discovery for all users. This flywheel deepens with scale.

0412-Month Pilot Plan

The seed funding will finance a focused, measurable 12-month pilot across three Lango districts — Lira, Alebtong, and Dokolo — targeting 12,000 farming households (10% of the sub-region). The pilot will run through one complete maize growing season, with baseline and endline surveys to establish impact metrics for Series A fundraising.

Q1
Months 1–3 · Infrastructure & Onboarding
Deploy Supabase geospatial data pipeline for all three districts. Finalise Aeryion satellite coverage via Google Earth Engine. Hire 18 district field agents. Begin Coltiva USSD onboarding with MTN and Airtel Uganda. Target: 3,000 active Coltiva users by end of Q1.
Q2
Months 4–6 · Growing Season Launch
Full Aeryion advisory activation aligned with maize planting season. Coltiva premium tier launch with weekly field reports. LinkTrade marketplace opens with 40 pre-verified buyers onboarded. Target: 8,000 active users, first 500 MT of forward contracts placed.
Q3
Months 7–9 · Harvest & Market
Peak LinkTrade activity as harvest season begins. Real-time price feeds and logistics coordination live. Aeryion harvest-readiness alerts deployed. Target: 12,000 active users, $600,000 in escrow trade volume processed.
Q4
Months 10–12 · Impact Assessment & Series A
Independent endline survey measuring yield, income, and market access outcomes against baseline. Publish impact report. Initiate Series A fundraising targeting $12M for national rollout. Target: $1.8M total LinkTrade volume for the year.
